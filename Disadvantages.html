All You Need to Know about Sleep

Memory Issues

Even just one night of less than 5 hours sleep can impair short-term memory. We ourselves are aware that the next day we have a hard time remembering things: 'Where did I leave my keys? What's that person's name? During sleep your brain forms connection that help you process and remember new information. A lack of sleep can negatively impact both short and long-term memory.

Mood Swings

Mood Swings are sudden changes in how you feel. Sleep Deprivation can make you moody, emotional, and quick-tempered. Chronic sleep deprivation can affect your mood and lead to anxiety or depression. If you don't get enough sleep, you might feel cranky. You will be angry more frequently and easily.

Trouble with Thinking and Concentration

Without enough sleep, your brain cells do not work as efficiently and you may have trouble concentrating. If you haven't sleep well, you may struggle to fully pay attention of what's in front of you or around you. You are also more likely to make poor choices throughout the day.

Weakened Immunity

Immune system is critical to overall health. It is fundamental to healing wounds, warding off infections, and protecting against chronic and life-threatening illnesses. Consistent sleep strengthens the immune system, allowing for balanced and effective immune function. Lack of sleep on the other hand, can impair the Immune system.

Weight Gain

The neurotransmitters Ghrelin promotes hunger, and Leptin contributes to feeling full. These two need to be regulated by getting enough sleep. The dysregulation of ghrelin and leptin may lead to increased appetite and diminished feeling of fullness. You will consume more calories than you need for a day. Sleeping also help the body to burn calories. Without enough sleep, the body can't perform this efficiently. The calories intake and the calories you use up each day will be unbalanced. This might lead you to be overweight or obese.

Risk for Diabetes and Heart Disease

Diabetes

If you don't get enough sleep, the body's ability to regulate glucose level is disrupted. Insulin is a hormone created by your pancreas that controls the amount of glucose in the bloodstream at any given moment. It also helps transfer glucose from the blood to the muscles, fat, and liver, and other cells. Insulin resistance occurs when the body has trouble producing insulin, or when the insulin fails to transfer glucose into these cells. This leads to a build-up of glucose in the blood, otherwise known as high blood sugar.

Heart Disaease

Heart is responsible for pumping blood throughout the body and powers the circulatory system that ensures that all the organs and tissues in the body get the oxygen they need. Sleep is an essential time for the body to recuperate. During the non-rapid eye movement (NREM) sleep stages, heart rate slows, blood pressure drop, and breathing stabilizes. These changes reduce stress on the heart, allowing it to recover from strain that occurs during waking hours. Without enough sleep, a person doesn't spend enough time in the deep stages of NREM sleep that benefit the heart. Chronic sleep deprivation is associated with numerous heart problems including high blood pressure, irregular heart rate, chest pain, heart attack and stroke.

Heart Attack and Stroke

A heart attack happens when the flow of blood to the heart gets blocked. Heart drops during the NREM sleep stages and then picks back up as you prepare to wake up. Sleep interruptions have also been linked to the potential for heart attacks. Because both heart rate and blood pressure can be negatively affected. Fragmented sleep can cause cardiac stress and may induce a heart attack. A stroke is when blood flow to the brain is cut off, causing brain cells to dies from lack of oxygen. Ischemic strokes occur when a blood clot or plaque blocks an artery. Sleep deprivation increases blood pressure, and high blood pressure is considered to be the leading risk factor for strokes. In addition, by contributing to plaque build-up in the arteries, insufficient sleep may make it easier for blockages to occur and cause mini-strokes or strokes.
