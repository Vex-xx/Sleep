All You Need to Know about Sleep

What is Sleep?

Sleep is the best way to take a rest. It doesn't only rest our body movement it also reduced some activity of the brain. There are some brain activities that needs to be done while we are asleep. Such as restoration of Muscular, Skeletal, Nervous, and Immune system. These all functions are important to maintain the mood and keep us active during daytime. It also help memorizing things better and keeping us healthy. So, we can learn quite well and help thinking to have better idea. Internal Alarm clocks of Human promotes sleep daily at night. It synchronized with the solar time (Solar time is the calculation of passage of time based on the position of sun in the sky). An adult should sleep 7-8 hours for a day. For children it is recommended around 10 hours. A new born baby needs 14-17 hours. Sleep is very important routine which you shouldn't be careless about. People who doesn't have enough sleep especially those who sleep less than 5 hours a day are most likely to suffer diabetes and heart related disease.

Sleep Stages

Sleeping doesn't mean the brain is totally inactive. Sleep occurs in repeating sleep cycle. There are two categories in sleep cycle. NREM (Non-Rapid Eyes movement) and REM (Rapid Eyes Movement). NREM is composed of three stages. Stage 1 (N1), Stage 2 (N2) and Stage 3 (N3). REM is the phase dreams most likely to happen. Each sleep cycle last 90 to 120 minutes. The duration gets longer as the night goes on. An adult have Four to Five sleep cycle which is enough sleep time for a day. An adult needs 7 - 8 hour sleeps time, Teenage needs 9 - 10 hours. Young Children needs 10 - 14 hours and Newborn baby needs 14 - 17 hours daily.

Stage 1

Stage 1 : N1 is the first stage a person enter right after he fell asleep. Also known as light sleep. In this stage, the body is not fully relaxed, though the body and brain activity are getting slow. It is easy to wake the person up. If this stage is not interrupted, it last about 7 minutes then the person enters the Stage 2(N2).

Stage 2

Stage 2 : N2 is also light sleep . During N2,there is a drop in body temperature, relaxed muscles, slowed breathing and heart rate. At the same time, brain waves show a new pattern and there are short bursts of activity that help resist being woken up by external stimuli.

N2 last 10 - 25 minutes. The duration becomes longer in each sleep cycle during the night. A person spent half of the sleep time in N2 sleep.

Stage 3

Stage 3 : N3 is the most important part of the sleep cycle. Also known as deep sleep. In this stage, Muscle tone, Pulse and Breathing rate decrease as the body relaxed even further. You need N3 sleep to wake up feeling rested. Without N3 sleep, you feel tired and drained even if you slept for a long time. If this happens you should go see the doctor.

N3 allows the brain to perform restoration of vital processes. Such as Muscle, Nervous, Skeletal and Immune system. These processes help to maintain the mood, think creatively, memorize better and keep us healthy. During the first sleep cycle, N3 last 20 to 40 minutes. As you continue sleeping, Duration of N3 gets shorter and REM longer instead.

REM

REM : Eyes moving rapidly under the eyelids. The Muscle is paralyzed. Most dreams happen in this phase. Dream is the succession of images, idea, emotion, and sensation. It takes 9min to 20min. During REM, the brain activity looks similar to that of while you are awake. Experts believe that REM is also essential for the brain. REM last 20 - 60 minutes. The duration is short in the first sleep cycle. But as the night goes on, it is getting longer.

The structure of a person's sleep stages and cycles is known as sleep architecture. Experts believe that each stage of sleep plays a part in a healthy sleep architecture that generates quality sleep.

Why is sleeping important?

Benefits of Sleep

Getting enough sleep has many benefits. It can help you:

  • Get sick less often
  • Stay at a healthy weight
  • Lower your risk for serious health problems, like diabetes and heart disease
  • Reduce stress and improve your mood
  • Think more clearly and do better in school and at work
  • Get along better with people

What could Happen if I don't get enough sleep?

The effects of Sleep Deprivation

The effects of sleep deprivation include:

  • Memory Issues
  • Mood Swings
  • Trouble with thinking and concentration
  • Weakened immunity
  • Weight gain
  • Risk of Heart Disease and Diabetes

Sleep Disorder

Sleep disorders involve problems with the quality, timing, and amount of sleep, which result in daytime distress and impairment in functioning.

How can I know if I have a sleep disorder?

Sleep disorder can cause many different problems. Common signs of sleep disorder include:

  • Trouble falling or staying asleep
  • Still feeling tired after a good night's sleep
  • Sleepiness during the day that makes it difficult to do everyday activities
  • Frequent loud snoring
  • Pauses in breathing or gasping while sleeping
  • Tingling or crawling feelings in your legs or arms at night that feel better when you move or massage the area
  • Feeling like it's hard to move when you first wake up

If you have any of these signs, talk to a doctor or nurse. You may need testing or treatment for specific problem of sleep disorder.

Insomnia

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that can make it hard to fall asleep or stay asleep. It also can cause you to wake up too early and not be able to get back to sleep. You may still feel tired when you wake up. Insomnia can drain your energy level and affect your mood. It also can affect your health, work performance. At some point, many adults have short-term insomnia. This can last for days or weeks. Short-term insomnia is usually due to stress or a distressing event. But some people have long-term insomnia, also called chronic insomnia. This lasts for three months or more. Insomnia may be the main problem, or it may be related to other medical conditions or medicines. You do not have to put up with sleepless nights. Simple changes in your daily habits often can help.

Symptoms

Insomnia symptoms may include:

  • Having a hard time falling asleep at night.
  • Waking up during the night.
  • Waking up too early.
  • Feeling tired or sleepy during the day.
  • Feeling cranky, depressed or anxious
  • Having a hard time paying attention, focusing on tasks or remembering.
  • Making more errors or having more accidents
  • Having ongoing worries about sleep.

Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a serious sleep disorder that happens when your breathing stops and starts while you're asleep. If it goes untreated, it can cause loud snoring, daytime tiredness, or more serious problems like heart trouble or high blood pressure.

This condition is different from regular, or primary, snoring. Primary snoring may be caused by nose or throat conditions, your sleep style (especially back sleeping), being overweight or older, or using alcohol or other depressants. While both types of snoring happen when tissues in the back of your throat vibrate, people with sleep apnea tend to:

  • Snore much more loudly than those with regular snoring
  • Pause for over 10 seconds while they breathe
  • Take shallow breaths, gasp, or choke
  • Be restless during sleep

Narcolepsy

Narcolepsy is a chronic neurological disorder that affects the brain's ability to control sleep-wake cycles. People with narcolepsy may feel rested after waking, but then feel very sleepy throughout much of the day. Many individuals with narcolepsy also experience uneven and interrupted sleep that can involve waking up frequently during the night.

Narcolepsy can greatly affect daily activities. People may unwillingly fall asleep even if they are in the middle of an activity like driving, eating, or talking. Other symptoms may include sudden muscle weakness while awake that makes a person go limp or unable to move (cataplexy), vivid dream-like images or hallucinations, and total paralysis just before falling asleep or just after waking up (sleep paralysis).

In a normal sleep cycle, a person enters rapid eye movement (REM) sleep after about 60 to 90 minutes. Dreams occur during REM sleep, and the brain keeps muscles limp during this sleep stage, which prevents people from acting out their dreams. People with narcolepsy frequently enter REM sleep rapidly, within 15 minutes of falling asleep. Also, the muscle weakness or dream activity of REM sleep can occur during wakefulness or be absent during sleep. This helps explain some symptoms of narcolepsy.

Some tips to fall asleep quickly

  • Make sure the room is dark enough and the surrounding is quiet or you can use earplugs to reduce the noises and blindfold to cover your eyes.
  • Taking your eyes off the screen (Phone, Tv, Pc etc.) 30 minutes or an hour before you sleep will help you fall asleep quickly as well.
  • There are also sleeping pills that help with your sleep, but it does have side effects. So, you should discuss with the doctor before taking sleeping pills.

How to deal with lack of sleep for a night or for a short time?

Drinking a cup of coffee will help you focus and being awake temporarily during daytime. Caffeine promotes wakefulness in the morning and to ward off sleepiness during the day. But it comes with potential side effects, including headaches, and nervousness. Keep in mind that not to drink coffee around your sleeping time, especially during nighttime. It will negatively impact your sleep quality.
